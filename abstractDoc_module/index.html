Industry

  • a variant is something a little different from others of the same type
  • being variant is differing from a norm or standard
  • a variant is an event that departs from expectations

  • A variant is a differing/ deviant/ abnormal execution (in contrast to an alternative decision between two options) or different occurrences of the same product line (i.e. a product variant)

  • product variants: different occurrences or instantiations of a product (have a common basis and differ in specific properties, to be distinguished from versions of a product)

    • definition by Microsoft "A product variant is a specific item that is grouped with related variants that together form a product. Variants usually vary from each other in one or more properties. For example, a medium-sized, green shirt with a stock-keeping unit (SKU) of 14678 is one product variant of the Shirt product; together size, color, and SKU form one variant. A product variant always includes a unique identifier, such as an SKU, and a price. Each product variant is based on the same product definition."
    • practical example or another example or this
  • software variants are product variants: share significant functionality but differ in specific features (e.g. Windows Home vs. Windows Professional)

  • SAP variant management:

    • objects (materials, equipments, or documents) are represented by Bills of Matial (BOM), SAP distinguishes ...
    • variant BOM groups (that group together several BOM that represent different objects but have a high proportion of identical parts: similar document structures are grouped into a variant BOM if they mostly have identical items, e.g. if they represent different versions of the same document)
    • and multiple BOMs'' (''collections of alternatives that group together several BOMs that describe one object with different combinations of materials for different processing methods)

  • context-sensitive customization'': customizing off-the-shelf software for an enterprise's individual operations and management produces ''software variants, e.g. C Janiesch et al. propose the adaptation of software in terms of its document: "document masters" (document templates for the generation of arbitrary texts during the product development life cycle) are annotated with configuration parameters that specify how to adapt the master given a certain context, the more specialized the master the more likely it can not be reused for other scenarios

  • customizing documents: Serial letters (or form letters) are documents sent to multiple recipients, they can be considered variation of a generic (default) letter. Address, title, and parts of the content are often personalized and allow the appearance of a personal letter. Text processing systems (like MS Office) that support serial letters are based on a database and a template. The database includes variable data (names, addresses, titles), which are integrated in the template. In addition, the database can provide information to adapt the letter for a particular audience by (1) filtering the list of recipients and (2) conditionalising the content for better individualization.

  • alternative necessitates a choice between mutually exclusive possibilities: either/ or (sy: mutually exclusive)

  • The alt attribute is used in HTML and XHTML documents to specify text that is to be rendered when the element to which it is applied cannot be rendered. For example, an alternative text is used as a replacement for an image, whenever the image cannot be seen.
